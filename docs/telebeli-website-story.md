# Telebeli — Website Story (Phase 03, v2)

**What changed from v1:** v1 defined the psychology of each beat but labeled beats with *internal* names ("Recognition," "The safety net") that fail the approval gate — read alone, they explain nothing. v2 assigns every beat a **reader-facing heading** that carries the message, so the section headings *in sequence tell the entire Telebeli story*. The Approval Gate at the end proves it.

**Locked to prior phases:** buyer = the accountable operator burned by a silent-failure pilot; spine = **watch · score · prove**; emotional target = **calm conviction, not hype**; every heading obeys the brand rules (no buzzwords, no exclamation marks, no fabricated metrics — "call #5,000" is a qualitative failure-mode framing, not a claimed number).

---

## 1. Website Story

**The premise, in one line:** a burned, skeptical operator arrives, is *understood*, is *shown* the product working, is walked through why it's different, has every fear answered in the order they feel it, is told the honest truth about what we can and can't prove — and only then is asked, gently, to take the smallest possible next step.

**The story is told by the section headings themselves.** These are the actual, reader-facing headings a visitor sees top to bottom. Read them in order and the product explains itself — that is the design requirement, not a nice-to-have.

| # | Reader-facing heading (what the visitor actually reads) |
|---|---|
| 0 | **Voice AI you can watch, score, and prove.** *(hero; support line: "AI takes calls. You grow.")* |
| 1 | **Most voice AI goes dark in production. You find out from the customer.** |
| 2 | **Watch a real call resolve — then hand off to a human.** |
| 3 | **See every call, live and after it happens.** |
| 4 | **Every call is scored, so problems surface before your customers feel them.** |
| 5 | **When a call shouldn't be handled by AI, it reaches a human — with full context.** |
| 6 | **It holds at call #5,000.** |

Each heading is a promise; the content beneath it is the proof. Nothing below a heading may introduce a second idea — one heading, one message, one question answered.

*Candor note on Beat 7:* the phrase "built for operations that can't fail quietly" is true today. Any stronger customer-proof heading (e.g., "trusted on live operations") ships **only** once the first real case study exists (roadmap H1). Until then, Beat 7 leads with the Sagenex fact, not an unbacked customer claim.

## 2. User Journey

The visitor's psychological path from arrival to certain — with the four journey checks answered inline.

**Arrival → Recognition → Demonstration → Understanding → Reassurance → Confidence → Trust → Integrity → Fairness → Action.**

- **Does every section answer one major question?** Yes. Each beat maps to exactly one live question (§6). A beat that answers zero questions is deleted; one that answers two is split.
- **Is there a logical progression?** Yes — and it is not arbitrary: *the section order is the buyer's objection order.* The visitor's mind produces these questions in this sequence, so the page simply follows it. This is why no beat can be re-ordered without answering a question the visitor hasn't asked yet.
- **Is curiosity maintained?** Yes. Every answer opens the next question (the open loop). Stopping mid-page feels like leaving a question of your own unanswered, so the visitor's own curiosity pulls the scroll — not our layout.
- **Is cognitive load minimized?** Yes, three ways: (1) one idea per section, always; (2) the *heading carries the message*, so a skimmer who reads only headings still understands (the approval gate); (3) depth (docs, full security, full pricing) lives on branch exits, never on the main path, so the primary scroll stays light while remaining complete for the reader who wants more.

## 3. Information Hierarchy

The priority order of what must be understood, independent of layout. If a visitor absorbed only the top of this list, they would still have the right idea of Telebeli.

1. **The one idea** — voice AI you can watch, score, and prove. *(Beat 0)*
2. **Why it matters** — black-box voice AI fails silently, and you're accountable. *(Beat 1)*
3. **Proof it's real** — a real call, watched. *(Beat 2)*
4. **The three mechanisms** — visibility → scoring → handoff. *(Beats 3–5)*
5. **Reliability, evidenced.** *(Beat 6)*
6. **Legitimacy** — Sagenex, and (when proven) real customers. *(Beat 7)*
7. **Compliance + candor.** *(Beat 8)*
8. **Commercial clarity.** *(Beat 9)*
9. **The next step.** *(Beat 10)*

**Rule:** nothing lower may appear before something higher. A visitor never meets pricing before proof, or a feature before the wound. The hierarchy is *obvious* precisely because the reader-facing headings (§1) render it visible at a skim.

## 4. Content Flow

Beat by beat: the reader-facing heading, the one question it answers, its single purpose, why it sits here and nowhere else, and the open loop that earns the next scroll.

**Beat 0 — "Voice AI you can watch, score, and prove."**
Q: *What is this, and am I in the right place?* · Purpose: qualify the ICP and state the one idea in under five seconds. · Why here: it's the door. · Opens: *do they understand why I'd need that?*

**Beat 1 — "Most voice AI goes dark in production. You find out from the customer."**
Q: *Do they understand my problem?* · Purpose: prove we understand the silent-failure wound before we sell anything. · Why here (first content beat): you cannot sell to a burned buyer until you name the burn; leading with their truth is the differentiated opening. · Opens: *fine — but do they actually fix it?*

**Beat 2 — "Watch a real call resolve — then hand off to a human."**
Q: *Is this real, or another slick demo?* · Purpose: convert skepticism by letting the visitor watch it work — real call, real handoff. · Why here: immediately after recognition, while "do they solve it?" is hottest; show before you tell. · Opens: *how is this different from the black box that burned me?*

**Beat 3 — "See every call, live and after it happens."** *(Pillar: Visibility)*
Q: *How is this different?* · Purpose: the category-defining difference — nothing is hidden. · Why here: differentiation must land while "how is this different" is hot. · Opens: *seeing it is nice — how do I know it's any good?*

**Beat 4 — "Every call is scored, so problems surface before your customers feel them."** *(Pillar: Accountability)*
Q: *How do I know the calls are good, and stay good?* · Purpose: open the black box — measured quality, caught regressions, auditable. · Why here: quality is the next worry after visibility. · Opens: *and the calls it shouldn't handle?*

**Beat 5 — "When a call shouldn't be handled by AI, it reaches a human — with full context."** *(Pillar: Graceful Handoff)*
Q: *What happens on the hard calls?* · Purpose: neutralize the most emotional objection (loss of control). · Why here: the scariest fear must clear before we imply scale or spend. · Opens: *does it hold at my volume?*

**Beat 6 — "It holds at call #5,000."** *(Reliability)*
Q: *Does it break quietly at scale?* · Purpose: reliability as a design commitment, evidenced by the surfaces just shown (you'd *see* the drift). Never paired with a fabricated uptime number. · Why here: the buyer now projects onto real volume. · Opens: *who else trusts this?*

**Beat 7 — "An initiative of Sagenex Group — built for operations that can't fail quietly."** *(Proof & legitimacy)*
Q: *Who trusts this, and who's behind it?* · Purpose: institutional weight now; real customer proof the moment it exists. · Why here: proof confirms after the mechanism already convinced. · Opens: *could I get this past compliance?*

**Beat 8 — "Built for compliance. Honest about what we can't prove yet."** *(Trust + candor)*
Q: *Can I put this in front of risk and compliance?* · Purpose: honest security posture, auditability, DPA/BAA path — plus the transparency moment that certifies every claim above it. · Why here: the compliance gate sits just before commercial. · Opens: *what will it cost, and will it surprise me?*

**Beat 9 — "Transparent pricing, billed by the second. No hidden stack."** *(Commercial)*
Q: *What will this cost?* · Purpose: remove the unpredictable-cost fear; transparency is the pitch. · Why here: commercial de-risking immediately before the ask. · Opens: *what's the low-risk next step?*

**Beat 10 — "See it on your lines. Talk to our team."** *(Invitation)*
Q: *What do I do next without regretting it?* · Purpose: the smallest, safest step — watch/hear/pilot before commit, with a human; plus a shareable kit for the internal champion. · Why last: the ask can only be made once trust clears the threshold. · Closes the loop → action, or an email capture (never a dead end).

**Structure checks:** no two beats answer the same question (Beat 2 = "is it real," Beat 3 = "can I keep seeing it," Beat 6 = "does it hold at scale" — distinct). The removed items (generic feature grid, "for every business" logos, fabricated metrics band, synthetic tour) are gone because none answered a live question.

## 5. Scroll Narrative

You arrive tired of demos, because one of them quietly failed on you. The page doesn't brag — it says *voice AI you can watch, score, and prove*, and then describes exactly how your last pilot died: flawless in the demo, drifting at volume, an escalation that never fired, a complaint you heard about days late with nothing in the logs. Something unclenches. They get it.

So you keep going: is this real? Instead of telling you, the page lets you *watch* — a real call resolving, then handing cleanly to a human. Now you want to know how it's different from the black box that burned you, and the answer is the whole point: you see every call, live and after. But seeing isn't enough, so it shows you every call is *scored*, that a regression would surface before your customers ever felt it. And the fear you hadn't said out loud — what about the calls it *shouldn't* handle — is answered before you finish forming it: it hands off, with everything the human needs.

You start projecting this onto your real volume, and the page meets you there: it's built so call #5,000 runs like the first, and you'd *see* it if it didn't. You want to know who's behind it — Sagenex Group, and real operations. You wonder about compliance, and the page does the thing no competitor does: it tells you what it can prove today and what it can't yet. That honesty is what finally lets you exhale. Only then does it talk money — plainly, by the second, no surprises. And when it asks you to act, it asks for the smallest step: see it on your lines, talk to a person.

You didn't get excited. You got certain. That was the point.

## 7. Trust Journey

Trust is a balance that compounds. The visitor arrives in **trust debt** (a prior vendor burned them); each beat makes a deposit; the demo can only be reached once the balance crosses the action threshold.

| Beat | Deposit | Type |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | "They know exactly what they are." | Clarity |
| 1 | "They understand my problem better than the last vendor." | Credibility |
| 2 | "It's real — I watched it." | Proof |
| 3–4 | "I understand how it works; it's not magic." | Comprehension |
| 5 | "It protects me on the hard calls." | Safety |
| 6 | "It won't quietly fall apart at my scale." | Confidence |
| 7 | "A real group stands behind it." | Legitimacy |
| 8 | "They admit what they can't prove yet." | **Integrity — the largest deposit** |
| 9 | "They won't surprise me on the bill." | Fairness |
| 10 | "The next step is low-risk and human." | Reassurance |

**The insight:** for this buyer, candor (Beat 8) is the highest-value deposit — admitting a limit *retroactively certifies every claim above it*. This is why it sits late, and why a single overclaim anywhere would bankrupt the whole journey. Trust rises monotonically; by Beat 10 it clears the threshold to act.

## Approval Gate — can someone explain Telebeli from headings alone?

The test: strip everything but the section headings and read them in order. If they explain the product, the IA is world-class. If they don't, the skeleton is broken.

> 1. Voice AI you can watch, score, and prove.
> 2. Most voice AI goes dark in production. You find out from the customer.
> 3. Watch a real call resolve — then hand off to a human.
> 4. See every call, live and after it happens.
> 5. Every call is scored, so problems surface before your customers feel them.
> 6. When a call shouldn't be handled by AI, it reaches a human — with full context.
> 7. It holds at call #5,000.
> 8. An initiative of Sagenex Group — built for operations that can't fail quietly.
> 9. Built for compliance. Honest about what we can't prove yet.
> 10. Transparent pricing, billed by the second. No hidden stack.
> 11. See it on your lines. Talk to our team.

**Read alone, these headings tell someone:** what Telebeli is (accountable voice AI), the problem it solves (silent production failure), that it's provably real (watch a call), the three mechanisms (see → score → hand off), that it holds at scale, who's behind it (Sagenex), that it's compliance-ready and candid, that pricing is transparent, and what to do next (see it, talk to us). **A reader who saw only the headings could explain Telebeli accurately. Gate passed.**

The standard this sets for the build: *if a new heading is ever added that a stranger couldn't understand on its own, or that doesn't advance the story, it does not ship.* The headings are the story; the content is the proof.

## Governance

1. **The headings are the story.** Every section heading must be self-explanatory and must advance the narrative. The headings-only readthrough must always pass.
2. **One heading, one question, one message.** No section introduces a second idea.
3. **Order = objection order.** Beats may change expression, never position.
4. **Objections before pricing.** Beats 1–8 clear every objection; pricing (Beat 9) and the ask (Beat 10) come only after.
5. **Candor is load-bearing.** Beat 8 certifies everything above it; protect it, and let no heading claim what isn't proven (see the Beat 7 candor note).
6. **No dead ends.** Every exit converts or captures an email.

*The visitor should never feel sold to. They should feel understood, then shown, then trusted with the truth — and arrive at the demo already certain.*
